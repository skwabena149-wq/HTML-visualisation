    • Why Cattle Born in 2020 Were Sold in 2021 and 2022

    Cattle born in 2020 spent their first months in sheltered conditions, either indoors or in controlled paddocks. This stage is crucial because young calves rely on milk and specialised feed, and are not yet strong enough for pasture grazing.

    By 2021, all these cattle had matured enough to graze outdoors. This is a typical progression in livestock management, although exact timing can vary depending on weather conditions, pasture availability, and the farm’s management strategies.

    Some of the cattle born in 2020 were sold in 2021. These were usually animals that:

    • Reached market weight earlier than others.
    • Were part of a stocking rate adjustment to prevent overgrazing.
    • Met customer or contract requirements at the right time.

    Others remained on the farm into 2022 because:

    • They needed more time to reach optimal weight or condition.
    • The farm manager chose to wait for more favourable beef market prices.
    • They were retained temporarily for breeding purposes before eventual sale.

    External factors such as seasonal demand, pasture growth cycles, and feed availability also influenced the sale timeline. By managing the timing of sales carefully, the farm aimed to balance animal welfare, pasture health, and financial returns.
